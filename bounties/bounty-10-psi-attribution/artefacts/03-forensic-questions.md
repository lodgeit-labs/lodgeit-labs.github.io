## Q3 (load-bearing) — Unrelated clients test sub-test (b): the "as a direct result" causation requirement

Section 87-20(1)(b) is not satisfied merely by the *existence* of a public offer. Its load-bearing wording is: *the individual or entity gained or produced the income from those services **as a direct result** of the individual or entity making offers or invitations (for example, by advertising) to the public at large or to a section of the public to provide the services.*

The test therefore has **two limbs, both of which must hold**:

1. **Offer limb** — did the taxpayer make offers or invitations to the public at large (or a section of it)? Advertising, a website, a public tender submission, and a LinkedIn profile can each qualify (per TR 2022/3 and *Commissioner of Taxation v Fortunatow* [2020] FCAFC 139).
2. **Causation limb ("as a direct result")** — for each contract counted toward the 80/20 threshold, was *that specific contract* obtained *as a direct result* of *that specific public offer*? A public offer that exists in parallel with — but is not the causal path to — the contract does not satisfy this limb. Word-of-mouth, family connection, and intermediary-mediated engagement each break the causal chain even if a LinkedIn profile or website exists.

Address each of the following, citing the relevant authority (statute, TR 2022/3, *Fortunatow*, and any ATO Interpretive Decisions you rely on):

1. What forms of public offer typically satisfy the **offer limb** in isolation?
2. What evidence (contemporaneous or otherwise) typically demonstrates the **causation limb** for a specific contract?
3. Consider four contract-acquisition modes and rule on whether each, standing alone, satisfies s 87-20(1)(b):
   (a) contract obtained via word-of-mouth from an existing client;
   (b) contract obtained via a family or personal connection;
   (c) contract obtained after the client found the taxpayer through their LinkedIn profile;
   (d) contract obtained through a third-party recruitment intermediary who placed the taxpayer with the client.
4. Where multiple public offers exist in parallel (e.g. website + LinkedIn + tender submissions), how does the weight of evidence for the causation limb accumulate?

_(Reviewers should cite the operative subsection, TR 2022/3, *Commissioner of Taxation v Fortunatow* [2020] FCAFC 139; 278 FCR 409, and any relevant ATO Interpretive Decisions.)_
